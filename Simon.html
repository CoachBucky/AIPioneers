Challenging Economic Theory

Bounded Rationality & Satisficing

Simon argued against the idea of a perfectly rational "economic man." He introduced two revolutionary concepts:

  • Bounded Rationality: Human decisions are limited ("bounded") by the information available, our cognitive limits, and time.
  • Satisficing: We don't seek the *optimal* solution. Instead, we choose the first option that is "good enough" or satisfactory.

This work earned him the 1978 Nobel Prize in Economics.

A Lasting Legacy in Modern AI

Simon's foundational ideas are alive in today's AI:

  • Heuristics: His focus on "rules of thumb" is central to how modern AI systems efficiently search for solutions, from GPS navigation to game-playing bots.
  • Resource Limits: The concept of bounded rationality mirrors the reality of modern AI, which must make decisions with finite computing power and data.
  • Cognitive Architecture: His work linking human psychology to computation paved the way for cognitive science and the quest to build more human-like AI.
