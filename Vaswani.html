Early Life & Education

  • Grew up in Oman and Nagpur, India, with an early fascination for science and math.
  • Studied Computer Science at Birla Institute of Technology, Mesra.
  • Moved to the U.S. and earned a Ph.D. in Computer Science from the University of Southern California (USC).
  • His research at USC focused on Natural Language Processing (NLP) and machine learning, where he recognized the potential of deep learning.

A Paradigm Shift: "Attention Is All You Need"

In 2017, while at Google Brain, Vaswani was the lead author of this seminal paper. It introduced the Transformer model, which challenged the dominance of sequential models like RNNs and LSTMs by relying solely on a mechanism called self-attention.

Read the Paper Watch: What is Attention?

Deconstructing the Transformer

  • Self-Attention: Allows the model to weigh the importance of all other words in a sequence simultaneously.
  • Parallel Processing: Can process an entire sequence at once, dramatically speeding up training compared to sequential RNNs.
  • Positional Encodings: Used to give the model information about the order of words, since it doesn't process them sequentially.

Impact on Modern AI

The Transformer is the foundational architecture for nearly all modern Large Language Models (LLMs). Its principles have powered a revolution in AI, with applications from search and translation to creative content and code generation.

This architecture has also been adapted for other domains, including computer vision (Vision Transformers) and biology (AlphaFold).
