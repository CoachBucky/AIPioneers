The Prodigy's Beginnings

  • Born in New York City (1927).
  • Attended Bronx High School of Science.
  • Served in the U.S. Navy in WWII.
  • B.A. in Mathematics from Harvard (1950).
  • Ph.D. in Mathematics from Princeton (1954).
  • Joined MIT in 1958, co-founding the AI Lab.

The "Society of Mind" Theory

In his 1986 book, Minsky proposed that intelligence isn't a single entity.

Instead, it emerges from the interactions of many simple, non-intelligent processes called "agents".

Each agent performs a specific task, and complex thought arises from their collaboration and conflict.

Building the Future

  • Invented the first head-mounted graphical display (a precursor to VR/AR).
  • Built early robotic hands with tactile sensors.
  • Created visual scanners for machines to "see."
  • Pioneered telepresence and robotics.

The "Perceptrons" Controversy

In 1969, Minsky & Papert's book "Perceptrons" proved the limitations of simple neural networks.

It showed they couldn't solve basic problems like XOR, leading to an "AI Winter" as funding for neural net research dried up.

However, this critique also pushed researchers to develop the multi-layered networks that power today's deep learning.

Minsky's Enduring Legacy

  • His work spurred the creation of multi-layer networks, the basis of Deep Learning.
  • The "Society of Mind" influences modern cognitive architectures and multi-agent systems.
  • His focus on "common sense reasoning" remains a key challenge in AI.
  • His early inventions paved the way for today's robotics, VR, and AR.
